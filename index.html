My name is Shujaut, and I am a doctoral candidate in Aerospace Engineering at Iowa State University. I work with Prof. Paul Durbin in the area of subgrid and hybrid RANS/LES modeling of scalar transport and transitional boundary layers respectively. In addition to my work in flow physics, I have also minored in Applied Scientific Computing and Astrophysics with substantial experience in numerical methods and fluid dynamic problems in astrophysical/compressible settings. aaaaaaaaaaaaI completed my Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering at University of Massachusetts Amherst with specialization in Computational Aero/Fluid Dynamics (CFD). I worked with Prof. Blair Perot partly as a Research Assistant in Computational Fluid Dynamics Laboratory and as a graduate student working towards my thesis. As a graduate student, during my master's degree, my research interests spanned the broad areas of numerical methods and applied CFD. As a major part of my stint as a Research Assistant in CFD Lab - UMass Amherst, I have tested a novel CFD code on a number of test cases for its improved speed, accuracy and convergence. My master's thesis focused on development of large scale flow altering devices that enhance the vertical transport of kinetic energy in wind farms and help mitigate the wake effect by displacing the wake out of the way of downstream turbines. I did my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Mechanical Engineering Department of Aligarh Muslim University, India - (AMU) which is one of the oldest and premier universities (ranked 7th by US News & World Report, 2016) in the country. My undergraduate thesis was primarily focused on the generic area of CFD of laminar, compressible flows, and involved the determination of the stalling characteristics and location of aerodynamic center of a novel 'cusped' leading edge airfoil. The objective behind the design was to mitigate the effect of the 'bow shock' and minimize the drag in comparison to conventional supersonic airfoils.
