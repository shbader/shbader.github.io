Hello, my name is Shujaut, and I am a doctoral candidate in Aerospace Engineering at Iowa State University. I work with Prof. Paul Durbin in the area of subgrid and hybrid RANS/LES modeling of scalar transport and transitional boundary layers respectively. In addition to my work in flow physics, I have also minored in Applied Scientific Computing and Astrophysics with substantial experience in numerical methods and fluid dynamic problems in astrophysical/compressible settings.

Prior to this, during my master's degree, I worked with Prof. Blair Perot at Theoretical and Computational Fluid Dynamics Laboratory, UMass Amherst. My master's thesis focused on the development of large scale flow altering devices that enhance the vertical transport of kinetic energy in wind farms and help mitigate the wake effect by displacing the wake out of the way of downstream turbines. I hold a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Aligarh Muslim University, India, which is one of the oldest and premier universities in the country. My undergraduate thesis, advised by Prof. Nadeem Hasan, was primarily focused on the generic area of CFD of laminar, compressible flows, and involved the determination of aerodynamic performance of a novel cusped leading edge airfoil. For more details about my research interests and work, please visit Projects and Publications. I blog about my work in fluid dynamics and numerical methods for computational physics at Flux.

In addition to my research in fluid dynamics and computational physics, I am also interested in the fundamental, philosophical questions about the nature of the universe and our place in it. In my spare time, I love to read heavily on the topics of philosophy of science, together with the academic study of the nature of theism and general religious belief. Contemplating life's big questions is what keeps me awake at night! I post my philosophical musings at backscatter.
