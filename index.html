Hi, my name is Shujaut, and I am a doctoral candidate in Aerospace Engineering at Iowa State University. I work with Prof. Paul Durbin in the area of subgrid and hybrid RANS/LES modeling of scalar transport and transitional boundary layers respectively. In addition to my work in flow physics, I have also minored in Applied Scientific Computing and Astrophysics with substantial experience in numerical methods and fluid dynamic problems in astrophysical/compressible settings.





Prior to this, during my master's degree, I worked with Prof. Blair Perot at the Theoretical and Computational Fluid Dynamics Laboratory, UMass Amherst. My master's thesis focused on development of large scale flow altering devices that enhance the vertical transport of kinetic energy in wind farms and help mitigate the wake effect by displacing the wake out of the way of downstream turbines. I hold a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Aligarh Muslim University, India, which is one of the oldest and premier universities in the country. My undergraduate thesis, advised by Prof. Nadeem Hasan, was primarily focused on the generic area of CFD of laminar, compressible flows, and involved the determination of aerodynamic performance of a novel cusped leading edge airfoil.
